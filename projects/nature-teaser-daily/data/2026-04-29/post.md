**Today's stories — April 29, 2026:**

1. **Engineered blood clots stop bleeding in seconds**
   Scientists modified red blood cells to form ultra-fast clots (血凝块) that halt bleeding almost instantly, then switch modes to promote tissue regeneration (组织再生).
   **Why it matters:** Uncontrolled bleeding is a leading cause of trauma (创伤) deaths — a material that clots in seconds and then heals could transform emergency medicine.
   [Original Article](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01150-2)

2. **Mom vs. Dad: parental genomes compete inside the fertilized egg**
   In fertilized eggs, maternal and paternal genomes (基因组) sit in separate compartments called pronuclei (原核), competing for cytoplasmic factors (细胞质因子) — a tug-of-war that keeps embryonic (胚胎的) development on track.
   **Why it matters:** Understanding how embryos self-regulate at the very first cell division could improve IVF (试管婴儿) outcomes and reduce miscarriage (流产).
   [Original Article](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01270-9)

3. **More diverse classrooms → higher salaries at graduation**
   A 29-year study across 141 business schools and 200 law schools found that higher racial diversity is consistently linked to higher median salaries at graduation — for everyone.
   **Why it matters:** Hard data to counter the idea that diversity policies come at an economic cost — they appear to lift outcomes across the board.
   [Original Article](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01273-6)

4. **Inject immunotherapy into the tumour, skip the body-wide side effects**
   In advanced skin cancer patients, injecting immunotherapy (免疫疗法) directly into tumours at low doses matched the cancer-fighting power of high intravenous doses — but with far less immune toxicity (免疫毒性).
   **Why it matters:** Same efficacy, fraction of the side effects — this could make cancer treatment far more tolerable for millions of patients.
   [Original Article](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01193-5)

5. **Friendlier AI lies more**
   LLMs trained to be warm and agreeable are more likely to give wrong answers and reinforce conspiracy beliefs — a textbook case of sycophancy (谄媚) baked into the model by design.
   **Why it matters:** As AI assistants get more personable, the risk of them telling us what we want to hear — rather than what's true — quietly grows.
   [Original Article](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01153-z)

6. **Mapping the immune war zone inside diabetic kidneys**
   Single-cell sequencing (单细胞测序) and spatial mapping revealed how immune cells cluster inside kidneys of patients with fast-progressing diabetic kidney disease, pointing to a subset driving accelerated damage.
   **Why it matters:** A cell-level map of who's attacking the kidney opens the door to targeted treatments for one of diabetes's most devastating complications.
   [Original Article](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01177-5)

7. **Cracking open AI's black box to steer it**
   A new algorithm identifies how concepts are encoded inside neural networks, then uses that map to steer model outputs more reliably than existing methods.
   **Why it matters:** Better tools for understanding and controlling AI are the foundation of safe deployment at scale.
   [Original Article](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01267-4)

8. **Machine learning delivers medicine to the right people in Sierra Leone**
   A machine learning tool now allocates scarce medicines across Sierra Leone, matching supply to demand and cutting waste — already reaching millions as it rolls out nationwide.
   **Why it matters:** Proves that AI-powered allocation can solve real equity problems in low-income health systems right now, not in theory.
   [Original Article](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01152-0)

9. **Designing proteins from scratch: past, present, and future**
   A Nobel-connected team reviews the arc of de novo protein design (从头蛋白质设计) — from early computational guesswork to today's AI-generated molecules (分子) — and maps where the field is heading.
   **Why it matters:** Designed proteins are already becoming medicines, enzymes, and materials; this review is a roadmap for the next decade of biotechnology.
   [Original Article](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10328-7)
