**1. Rhymes on Reason: Scientific Units Inspire Poetry**

Nature dipped into its archive this week with a playful challenge: readers were invited to write couplets about SI units (国际单位制) — the kilogram, the second, the mole, the candela. Precision dressed in verse. The same archive dive surfaced a report on an Anglo-Saxon burial ground (墓地), where artifacts are quietly rewriting what we know about early medieval England and the people who lived there.

**Why it matters:** Science communication works best when it escapes the lab. Asking readers to write poetry about measurement (测量) is a small, radical act — it collapses the distance between specialist knowledge and everyday curiosity. And archaeology (考古学) keeps reminding us that the past is never fully excavated.
